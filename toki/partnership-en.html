The hidden challenge your clients face

Beyond AI, DX, and ESG lies a more fundamental question

Digital services are optimized for "now"

Cloud storage and social platforms are superb at everyday recording and sharing. But they are designed for present-day convenience, not for preservation on a 100- or 1,000-year timescale. Different time horizons require fundamentally different design.

End-of-life planning is now a societal issue

Tens of thousands of graves go unclaimed each year in Japan. By 2040, single-person households will exceed 40% of all homes. "Who preserves memory?" has shifted from a personal concern to a social one.

The "millennium layer" does not yet exist

Digital estate management, virtual cemetery visits, AI recreations of the deceased — the market is growing fast. Yet every solution runs on top of today's infrastructure. What's needed is a complementary layer that endures independently — for a thousand years.
